# Workflow: Pre-Production

This workflow outlines the essential steps for the pre-production phase of an AI filmmaking project, focusing on establishing a strong foundation before any generation begins.

## Objective:
To define the core concept, characters, and visual style of the film, ensuring a consistent and well-planned approach to the generation phase.

## Key Activities:

### 1. Concept & Narrative Development
The first step is to solidify the core idea of the film. This involves writing a brief synopsis, defining the genre, and outlining the main narrative arc. Even for short, experimental films, having a clear beginning, middle, and end is crucial. This stage also includes drafting a script or a detailed shot list, which will serve as the blueprint for the generation phase.

### 2. Character Design & World-Building
Following the "character-first" approach, this is a critical step. Use the `CHARACTER_TEMPLATE.md` to create detailed profiles for all main characters. Focus not just on their physical appearance, but also their personality, backstory, and key props. This detailed information will be used to train the AI and ensure consistency across different shots. Simultaneously, define the key locations and the overall rules of the world your film inhabits.

### 3. Visual Style & Reference Gathering
Establish a consistent visual language for the film. This includes determining the color palette, lighting style (e.g., cinematic, high-key, moody), and overall aesthetic (e.g., cyberpunk, vintage, photorealistic). Gather reference images, mood boards, and even short video clips that exemplify the desired look and feel. These references will be invaluable when crafting prompts.

### 4. Prompt Strategy Planning
Before generating any final shots, develop a strategy for your prompts. Create a library of base prompts for your characters, locations, and styles. Plan how you will combine these elements to create specific scenes. Consider the camera angles, movements, and lighting required for each shot in your script or shot list.

## Deliverables:
- Film Synopsis and Script/Shot List
- Completed Character Sheets (saved in the `Characters` directory)
- Visual Style Guide and Mood Board
- Initial Prompt Library (saved in the `Prompts` directory)

## Notes & Learnings:
Investing time in pre-production significantly reduces frustration during the generation phase. A well-defined character sheet is the most important tool for maintaining consistency.
